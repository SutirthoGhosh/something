Success Stories: Under‑18 Rural Indian Tech Innovators

1. YTSAT 2.0: Helium Balloon Satellite for Farmers (Odisha)

Class 10 students Chiranjibi and Digbijay from Soro, Odisha, built a helium‑balloon satellite fitted with sensors to collect live farm data on soil, water, and air. It helps smallholders choose crops intelligently, boosting yields and livelihoods.

2. Rural Ethanol from Waste (Odisha)

Kaushik Kar of Soro and Sridhar Jena of Berhampur created a machine that converts dry agricultural waste into ethanol fuel—unlocking sustainable energy and income for rural villages.

3. Masala GPT: AI‑Powered Spice Mixer (Odisha)

P Aditya Das and Soumya Saurabh invented an AI device that blends over 500 regional spice mixes from 10 ingredients—preserving culinary tradition and enabling custom spice crafting at home.

4. Nagpur AI‑Driven Anganwadi (Maharashtra)

India’s first AI‑powered Anganwadi centre in Nagpur uses VR, smart dashboards, and digital content to transform early childhood learning in rural areas—with 40 more centres planned.

5. Avidus: Multilingual Learning Platform (Maharashtra)

At 17, Rajlaxmi Chavan built “Avidus”, a free multilingual education platform offering offline courses in Marathi, Hindi & English on finance, civil rights, health—and reaching rural learners across India.

6. Circadian AI: Detecting Heart Failure in 7 Seconds (Andhra)

14‑year‑old Siddharth Nandyala developed an AI app that detects early signs of heart failure in under 7 seconds using a smartphone’s mic and cloud ML analytics—a pro‑healthcare innovation.

7. Rural Tech Rise: VR, AI & Entrepreneurship Training (Tamil Nadu)

Teamled by school students (classes 7–9), Rural Tech Rise introduced rural children to VR headsets, ethical hacking, AI, and entrepreneurship, even hosting Shark Tank‑style pitch rounds to launch micro‑ventures.

8. Tele‑SNCU: Digital Neonatal Care (Maharashtra)

AIIMS‑Nagpur’s Tele‑SNCU connects remote tribal hospitals to specialists via IoT, dashboards and live video monitoring—halving neonatal mortality and improving outcomes in Maharashtra’s tribal zones.

9. Tata Steel Astronomy Winner (Keonjhar, Odisha)

Bhawani Bankula, a 15‑year‑old tribal student, won the Tata Young Astronomer Talent Search and visited ISRO centres in Bengaluru—making space science accessible and inspiring rural girls in science.

10. “Prithvi Rakshak”: Smart Vermicomposter (Punjab)

Two Class XII students from Ludhiana—Abhishek Dhanda & Prabhkirat Singh—built an AI + IoT composting machine that turns organic waste into compost in 38 days, 3x faster than usual, generating ₹1‑3 lakh/month.
